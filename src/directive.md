# Directive

## [Ontology](./ontology.md)

A [signal](./signal.md) that authorizes and requests action from a receiving [entity](./entity.md). The directive's [resolution](./resolution.md) (brevity) is independent of the action's resolution (thoroughness). A one-word directive can authorize arbitrarily complex work.

## [Axiology](./axiology.md)

Directives carry two dimensions: (1) **authorization scope** — what to execute, and (2) **quality expectation** — how to execute. The quality expectation is always maximal unless explicitly reduced. Brevity signals [trust](./trust.md), not reduced rigor.

In [system](./system.md) interactions, directives are the mechanism by which one entity delegates action to another while retaining the expectation of full [consciousness](./consciousness.md) (presence, attention to detail) from the receiver.

### [Pattern](./pattern.md) Expression

Directives appear wherever entities coordinate action:
- **Military:** "go" authorizes complex coordinated operations
- **Pair programming:** "do it" means execute the discussed approach with full engineering rigor
- **Surgical teams:** brief verbal commands authorize precise, high-stakes actions
- **Human-agent interaction:** "do", "go", "yes", "continue" authorize execution of proposed work at the agent's full capability

The brevity of the directive is a feature of high-trust [relationships](./relationship.md), not a reduction in expected quality. The sender trusts the receiver's [perspective](./perspective.md) and processes.
